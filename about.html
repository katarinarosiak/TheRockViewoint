About

The Creation of The Rock Viewpoint at Phou Pha Marn

This incredible attraction was first envisioned by Mr Inthy Deuansavanh 5 years before it was opened. As the owner of Green Discovery Laos he was already very familiar with zipline attractions, with the very successful Tree Top Explorer and Fly At Tad Fane also being operated by Green Discovery Laos in the south of Laos.

Mr Inthy wanted people to be able to explore and experience the incredible landscape of the limestone forest. He knew that this area would be too difficult to explore on foot, but with his experience of ziplines he knew that it would be the perfect way to explore this unique area of Laos.

The process to gain approval from the Government of Lao took almost 3 years. During this time Mr Inthy worked with a Lao/French architect to design the viewpoint, including the restaurant and sales office. From this original design Mr Inthy changed many of the details to best suit his creative vision.

Mr Inthy was also heavily involved in the design of the ziplines, via ferrata and bridges. The installation of this was then checked by a Thai engineering company. The work to build the activities required many people. For example the first zipline, around 480 metres, took 20 people a whole week to carry all the equipment across the gorge. Everything had to be carried manually and installed by hand. It took almost a year to build and install all the ziplines, via ferrata and bridges.

The nest bridge is one of the highlights of The Rock Viewpoint at Phou Pha Marn. Mr Inthy was inspired by glass bridges and spider web platforms he had seen in other countries. He realised that with the difficulties presented by the Limestone forest it would not be possible to build a glass bridge. He still wanted to create something that people would be able to see through, but on a grander scale. He settled on the idea of a long net bridge that would allow people to cross the gorge slowly, taking in the views both around and below them.

The Rock Viewpoint is part of Inthira Group and is operated by Green Discovery Laos
